More Information on Websites

  1. https://www.khanacademy.org/
    Khan Academy is a non-profit organization that offers great resources on many subjects from k-12 and beyond.
  2. https://www.exploratorium.edu/snacks
    The exploatorium is based in San Francisco, CA, and it is a museum of arts, science, and technology. They offer mini science experiments that can be incorporated with distance learning.

  3. https://edpuzzle.com/
    This is a great interactive website where students are able to watch videos and answer questions as the video progresses. Teachers are able to monitor the percentage of video that is watched and create questions for any video on YouTube.
  4. https://edu.google.com/products/classroom/?modal_active=none
    Google Classroom is a great resource to create a classroom environment for students.

  5. https://www.google.com/docs/about/
    Google Docs is an excellent way for students to be able to turn in assignments. They can write essays, answer individually assigned documents, or collaborate with other students by using Google Docs.

  6. sites.google.com
    Google Sites is an excellent resource to use. Teachers can develop personal websites with School District provided e-mails. Google Sites allows individuals to create websites with a template style website creator.

  7. jamboard.google.com
    The online Google Jamboard allows teachers to create an interactive way for students to collaborate with each other.
  8. YouTube.com
    YouTube has many educational videos that allows teachers to find and assign videos to watch. I recommend utilizing Edpuzzle to assign the YouTube video found so it is easier to track progress and grade.
